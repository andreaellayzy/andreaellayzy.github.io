Investigating Disparities in Measles Vaccination Coverage and Measles Incidence
Author

Ziying Yang

Research Question

Measles is an extremely contagious and airborne disease caused by a virus, it poses a grave threat to young children and often leads to severe complications and fatalities. The advent of measles vaccination has been instrumental in preventing 56 million deaths between 2000 and 2021 (World Health Organization 2023). Despite the existence of a safe and cost-effective vaccine, in 2021, there is still an alarming estimated global count of 128,000 measles-related deaths, predominantly affecting children under the age of 5, among them, most were either unvaccinated or inadequately vaccinated (World Health Organization 2023). Measles can lead to severe consequences, including death, remains a significant concern. Pregnant women who lack vaccination are also susceptible (World Health Organization 2023).

Here is some epidemiological background about measles:

(Our World in Data 2023)

Here is a general review about the effect of vaccination related to measles:

(Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) 2018)

There exists huge inequality on the measle vaccination coverage and measles related mortality over the world. This descriptive analysis aims to visually depict this inequality, offering the audience a fundamental understanding of the impact of measles vaccination. To effectively address the aforementioned question, I propose dividing it into three distinct parts.

1. Global Analysis of Measles Mortality and Vaccination Coverage:

  • What is the current status of measles incidence rates worldwide?

  • How does vaccination coverage vary across continents?

2. Country-Specific Examination:

  • What is the prevalence and impact of measles in India, Nigeria, China, and the United States?

  • How do vaccination coverage and mortality rates differ among these selected countries?.

3. Impact on Vulnerable Populations:

  • How has the decline in global measles vaccination coverage since 2008 affected unvaccinated or under-vaccinated populations?

  • What is the specific impact on children under the age of 5 and pregnant women in terms of measles incidence and mortality?

Audience

Health officials, policymakers, and practitioners seeking insights into measles trends, vaccination coverage, and mortality rates to inform public health strategies.

Subsequently, I generated two visual representations: one illustrating the distribution of measles in individual countries for the year 2022, and the other depicting the global measles trend from 1980 to 2022.
